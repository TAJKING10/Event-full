Abu Simbel Sun Festival

Egypt

The Abu Simbel Sun Festival in Egypt is a captivating cultural event that celebrates the ancient marvel of the Abu Simbel temples. Held annually on February 22nd and October 22nd, this festival commemorates the remarkable feat of ancient engineering where sunlight penetrates the inner sanctum of the temple, illuminating statues of gods and pharaohs deep within.
Dating back over 3,000 years, the Abu Simbel temples were built by Pharaoh Ramses II to immortalize his reign and honor the gods. The festival pays homage to this architectural wonder and the astronomical precision of its construction. As the sun rises, its rays align perfectly to illuminate the inner sanctuary, casting light upon the statues of Ramses II, Amun-Ra, and Ra-Horakhty.

Beyrouth Livres

Lebanon

Beirut's Livres Lebanon festival is a celebration of literature, knowledge, and the enduring spirit of intellectual curiosity. Held annually in the vibrant capital of Lebanon, this festival attracts bibliophiles, writers, and thinkers from around the world, creating a melting pot of ideas and inspiration.

UAE National Day Festival

United Arab Emirates

The UAE National Day is a momentous occasion that commemorates the unification of the seven emirates into one nation on December 2nd, 1971. It is a day of great pride and celebration for the people of the United Arab Emirates, marking the birth of a modern, prosperous, and forward-thinking nation.
Throughout the UAE, the National Day is celebrated with immense fervor and enthusiasm, with streets adorned in the colors of the national flag red, green, white, and black. Buildings are illuminated with spectacular light displays, and the air is filled with the sounds of traditional music and joyous festivities.
The National Day celebrations encompass a wide range of events and activities, including parades, fireworks displays, cultural performances, and exhibitions.

Jerash Festival

Jordan

The Jerash Festival in Jordan is a prestigious cultural event that celebrates the rich heritage and vibrant arts scene of the region. Held annually in the ancient city of Jerash, this festival attracts artists, performers, and spectators from across the globe, offering a diverse program of music, dance, theater, and visual arts.
The festival takes place amidst the breathtaking ruins of Jerash, one of the best-preserved Roman cities in the world. Against this magnificent backdrop, audiences are treated to a dazzling array of performances that showcase the talents of both local and international artists. From traditional Jordanian folk music to contemporary dance and theatrical productions, the Jerash Festival offers something for everyone, catering to a wide range of tastes and interests.
